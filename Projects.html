Integration

API vs SFTP vs Log Shipping

Enterprise systems exchange data in different ways depending on the need, frequency, volume, and system capability.

API is commonly used when systems need to communicate directly and quickly. SFTP is commonly used when files are transferred between systems in a controlled manner. Log shipping is typically used when database or system logs are moved for replication, backup, or downstream processing.

Choosing the right integration method depends on business requirements, security standards, near real-time needs, and technical feasibility.

Learning Databricks as a Beginner

Databricks can feel complex at first because it combines notebooks, SQL, compute, data catalogs, and collaborative development in one place.

For a beginner, the best way to learn is to start with simple SQL queries, understand tables and schemas, and then slowly move into views, transformations, testing, and production-style workflows.

The biggest learning is that data engineering is not only about writing queries. It is about understanding source data, cleaning it, validating it, and making it available for business teams in a reliable way.

Bridging Business and Technology

Successful technology delivery depends on more than tools. It requires clarity between what the business wants and what technical teams can build.

A strong project coordinator or technology consultant helps translate requirements, track decisions, simplify communication, and keep stakeholders aligned.

This bridge role is valuable because it reduces confusion, improves execution, and ensures that technology work stays connected to real business outcomes.
